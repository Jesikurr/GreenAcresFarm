Blueberries

You Pick Blueberries at Green Acres Blueberry Farm

Quick Facts

  • Flavor: Sweet with a light tartness when fully ripe.
  • Best color: Deep blue with a dusty looking white bloom on the skin.
  • Use for: Fresh eating, baking, freezing, smoothies, jams, and syrups.

How To Pick

  • Look for berries that are fully blue with no green or red at the stem end.
  • Gently roll a cluster in your hand. Ripe berries will fall off easily.
  • Avoid squeezing the berries. Let them drop into your bucket to prevent bruising.

How To Store

  • Do not wash the berries until you are ready to eat or cook them.
  • Pour berries into a shallow container so they are not crushed under their own weight.
  • Store uncovered or loosely covered in the refrigerator.
  • Best flavor is usually within 3 to 7 days.

Freezing Tips

  1. Spread unwashed berries in a single layer on a baking sheet.
  2. Freeze until solid, then transfer to labeled freezer bags or containers.
  3. Rinse just before using them in recipes or smoothies.
Tip: Freezing on a tray first keeps the berries from sticking together in one big block.

Fun Facts

  • Blueberries are rich in antioxidants and vitamin C.
  • They are one of the few fruits native to North America.

Easy Recipe Ideas

  • Blueberry Yogurt Bowl Add fresh or frozen berries to yogurt with a drizzle of honey and a sprinkle of granola.
  • Simple Blueberry Sauce Simmer blueberries with a bit of sugar and a splash of water until thick. Spoon over pancakes or ice cream.
  • Freezer Blueberry Muffins Stir berries into your favorite muffin batter. Bake and freeze muffins for quick breakfasts.
